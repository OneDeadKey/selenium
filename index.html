Selenium

A configurable Arsenik variant for split keyboards.




From 42 to 34 Keys (and beyond)

Like Arsenik, Selenium allows a step-by-step approach:

  • EZ: no hold-taps.
    • a beginner-friendly keymap for 42-key keebs
  • TT: thumb-taps. This is where it gets comfy:
    • access the `Nav` layer with a long press on the Space key
    • do Backspace and Return with a quick tap on thumb keys
  • HRM: homerow-mods. A sane default:
    • all modifiers are on homerow keys, nice and symmetricalc
    • all layer-shifts are on thumb keys
  • 2TK: two thumb keys.

These 4 modes share the same layers: only the thumb clusters change.

Hold-Taps That Just Work

Unreliable hold-taps are very frustrating. We believe Selenium fixes that.

Timing is Key

The common approach for compact keyboard keymaps is to use a single behavior for all hold-taps, which is often a compromise between “tap-preferred” and “hold-preferred” (like QMK’s “permissive hold”), and then rely on mitigating measures and fine timing adjustments to limit the number of typos.

In our experience, this doesn’t work reliably for most users and never will, because homerow-mods and thumb modifiers have two mutually excusive goals. Instead, Selenium uses three distinct kinds of hold-tap keys:

Tap-Preferred
the Space key and homerow-mods must be pressed at least 300ms to become a modifier / layer-shift
Hold-Preferred
Enter or Backspace must be tapped quickly (150ms by default)
Sticky
the Shift and Symbols keys behave as one-time-shifts when tapped, and as regular modifiers / layer-shifters when hold.

The main point in this approach is that you’ll never have to think of it: as long as your keymap respects the proper behavior/key associations, it just works.
