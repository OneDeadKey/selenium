Hold-Taps That Just Work

Unreliable hold-taps are very frustrating. We believe Selenium fixes that.

Timing is Key

The common approach for compact keyboard keymaps is to use a single behavior for all hold-taps, which is often a compromise between “tap-preferred” and “hold-preferred” (like QMK’s “permissive hold”), and then rely on mitigating measures and fine timing adjustments to limit the number of typos.

In our experience, this doesn’t work reliably for most users and never will, because homerow-mods and thumb modifiers have two mutually excusive goals. Instead, Selenium uses three distinct kinds of hold-tap keys:

Tap-Preferred:
behaves as a letter key by default, becomes a modifier / layer-shift when pressed longer than 300ms.
Perfect for Space and homerow-mods.
Hold-Preferred:
behaves as a modifier / layer-shift by default, becomes an input when pressed and released in less than 150ms.
Perfect for adding actions like Enter, Backspace, Tab, Esc to any thumb modifier / layer-shift.
Sticky:
behaves as a one-time-shift when tapped, and as a modifier / layer-shift when held.
Perfect for Shift and Symbols.

With this approach, you’ll never have to think of it: as long as your keymap respects the proper behavior/key associations, it just works. No unwanted shortcuts while typing text, no accidental Return while chatting.

Thumb-Shifting

Another opiniated choice with Selenium is that the Shift and Symbols keys belong to the thumbs, not to the homerow:

  • this makes it much easier to chain several uppercase letters or symbols;
  • these keys must have “hold-preferred” priority, which is not suitable for homerow-mods.

For keyboards with three good keys per thumb, Selenium sets sticky Shift and Symbols keys under the thumbs. These keys behave as one-time-shift by default, which gives additional safety to capitalize only the first letter of a word. The faster you type, the more you’ll see the benefits of such details.
